§ Internal versus External semidirect products

Say we have an inner semidirect product. This means we have subgroups N,KN, KN,K such that NK=GNK = GNK=G, NNN normal in GGG and N∩K={e}N \cap K = \{ e \}N∩K={e}. Given such conditions, we can realize NNN and KKK as a semidirect product, where the action of KKK on NNN is given by conjugation in GGG. So, concretely, let's think of NNN (as an abstract group) and KKK (as an abstract group) with KKK acting on NNN (by conjugation inside GGG).
