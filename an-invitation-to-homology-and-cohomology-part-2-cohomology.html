§ Exploring the structure of functions defined on the edges

Here, we try to understand what functions defined on the edges can look like, and their relationship with the ddd operator. We discover that there are some functions ge:E→Rg_e: E \rightarrow \mathbb Rge​:E→R which can be realised as the differential of another function gv:V→Rg_v: V \rightarrow \mathbb Rgv​:V→R. The differential forms such as geg_ege​ which can be generated a gvg_vgv​ through the ddd operator are called as exact differential forms. That is, ge=dgvg_e = d g_vge​=dgv​ exactly, such that there is no "remainder term" on applying the ddd operator. We take an example of a differential form that is not exact, which has been defined on the edges of the triangle above. Let's call it heh_ehe​. It is defined on the edges as:

This failure of locally consistent choices to be globally consistent is the essence of cohomology.

§ Cohomology of half-filled butterfly
