§ Proof of Heine Borel from Munkres

We wish to show that compact iff closed and bounded in R\mathbb RR.
  • Let SSS be closed and bounded. We wish to show that SSS is compact. Since SSS is bounded,SSS is contained in some large closed interval III. (1) Closed intervals are compact in the order topology of acomplete total order, and thus III is compact (R\mathbb RR is a complete total order).Then (2) SSS is a closed subset of a compact set III, and is thus compact.Hence closed and bounded implies compact.
  • Let SSS be compact. We wish to show that SSS is closed and bounded. Cover SSS with open balls of increasingradii. This is an open cover; Extract a finite subcover. From the finite subcover, pick the largest open ballIII. SSS is entirely contained in III, and is thus bounded. (3) SSS is closed, as it is a compact subset of a Haussdorff space. Hence, we are done.
