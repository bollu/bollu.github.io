§ Proof of minkowski convex body theorem

We can derive a proof of the minkowski convex body theorem starting from Blichfeldt’s theorem.

§ Blichfeldt's theorem

This theorem allows us to prove that a set of large-enough-size in any lattice will have two points such that their difference lies in the lattice. Formally, we have:
  1. A lattice L(B)≡{Bx:x∈Zn}L(B) \equiv \{ Bx : x \in \mathbb Z^n \}L(B)≡{Bx:x∈Zn} for some basis B∈RnB \in \mathbb R^nB∈Rn. The lattice LLL is spanned by integer linear combinations of rows of BBB.
  2. A body S⊆RnS \subseteq R^nS⊆Rn which need not be convex!, which has volume greater than det⁡(B)\det(B)det(B). Recall that for a lattice L(B)L(B)L(B), the volume of a fundamental unit / fundamental parallelopiped is det(B)det(B)det(B).
Blichfeldt's theorem tells us that there exists two points x1,x2∈Sx_1, x_2 \in Sx1​,x2​∈S such that x1−x2∈Lx_1 - x_2 \in Lx1​−x2​∈L.

§ Proof

The idea is to:
  1. Chop up sections of SSS across all translates of the fundamental parallelopiped that have non-empty intersections with SSS back to the origin. This makes all of them overlap with the fundamental parallelopiped with the origin.
  2. Since SSS has volume great that det⁡(B)\det(B)det(B), but the fundamental paralellopiped only has volume det⁡(B)\det(B)det(B), points from two different parallelograms must overlap.
  3. "Undo" the translation to find two points which are of the form x1=l1+δx_1 = l_1 + \deltax1​=l1​+δ, x2=l2+δx_2 = l_2 + \deltax2​=l2​+δ. they must have the same δ\deltaδ since they overlapped when they were laid on the fundamental paralellopiped. Also notice that l1≠l2l_1 \neq l_2l1​=l2​ since they came from two different parallograms on the plane!
  4. Notice that x1−x2=l1−l2∈L≠0x_1 - x_2 = l_1 - l_2\in L \neq 0x1​−x2​=l1​−l2​∈L=0, since we already argued that l1≠l2l_1 \neq l_2l1​=l2​. This gives us what we want.
