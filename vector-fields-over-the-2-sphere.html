§ 1. Vector fields over the sphere is not free

  • 1. Given two bundles E,FE, FE,F over any manifold MMM, a module isomorphism f:X(E)→X(F)f: \mathfrak X(E) \rightarrow \mathfrak X(F)f:X(E)→X(F) of vector fields as C∞(M)C^\infty(M)C∞(M) modules is induced by a smooth isomorphism of vector bundles F:E→FF: E \rightarrow FF:E→F.
  • 2. The module X(M)\mathfrak X(M)X(M) is finitely generated as a C∞C^\inftyC∞ module over MMM.
  • Now, assume that X(S2)\mathfrak X(S^2)X(S2) is a free module, so we get that X(S2)≃⊕iC∞(S2)\mathfrak X(S^2) \simeq \oplus_i C^\infty(S^2)X(S2)≃⊕i​C∞(S2).
  • By (2), we know that this must be a finite direct sum for some finite NNN: mathfrakX(S2)=⊕i=1NC∞(Sn)mathfrak X(S^2) = \oplus_i=1^N C^\infty(S^n)mathfrakX(S2)=⊕i​=1NC∞(Sn).
  • But having NNN different independent non-vanishing functions on S2\mathbb S^2S2 is the same as clubbing them all together into a vector of NNN values at each point at S2S^2S2.
  • So we get a smooth function S2→RnS^2 \rightarrow \mathbb R^nS2→Rn, AKA a section of the trivial bundle Rn‾≡S2×Rn\underline{\mathbb R^n} \equiv S^2 \times \mathbb R^nRn​≡S2×Rn.
  • This means that we have managed to trivialize the vector bundle over the sphere if vector fields over S2S^2S2 were a free module.
  • Now, pick the element S2×{(1,1,1,1,… )}∈S2×RnS^2 \times \{ (1, 1, 1, 1, \dots) \} \in S^2 \times \mathbb R^nS2×{(1,1,1,1,…)}∈S2×Rn. This is a nowhere vanishing vector field over S2S^2S2. But such an object cannot exist, and hence vector fields over the sphere cannot be free.
