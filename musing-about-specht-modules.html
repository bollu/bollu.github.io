§ Musing about Specht modules

If we generalize, to each point xxx, we are creating a group of orthogonal matrices OxO_xOx​ (like CtC_tCt​), such that
  • All points in the orbit have (the same/similar, unsure?) CtC_tCt​
  • For points outside the orbit, we evaluate to zero.
At least in dim=2, we can't take rotations (of finite order) as elements of OxO_xOx​. These behave like nth roots of unity on averaging, so we get 1+ω+ω2+⋯+ωn−1=01 + \omega + \omega^2 + \dots + \omega^{n-1} = 01+ω+ω2+⋯+ωn−1=0, leading to not giving any new points in OxO_xOx​.
