§ The ring of invariants

Rotations of R3\R^3R3 We have a group SO(3)SO(3)SO(3) which is acting on a vector space R3\mathbb R^3R3. This preserves the length, so it preserves the polynomial x2+y2+z2x^2 + y^2 + z^2x2+y2+z2. This polynomial x2+y2+z2x^2 + y^2 + z^2x2+y2+z2 is said to be the invariant polynomial of the group SO(3)SO(3)SO(3) acting on the vector space R3\mathbb R^3R3. But how do we attach meaning to the symbols x,y,zx, y, zx,y,z? Well, we can formulate them as linear operators on the vector space : x,y,z:R3→Rx, y, z : \mathbb R^3 \rightarrow \mathbb Rx,y,z:R3→R x(a,b,c)≡ax(a, b, c) \equiv ax(a,b,c)≡a, y(a,b,c)≡by(a, b, c) \equiv by(a,b,c)≡b, and z(a,b,c)≡cz(a, b, c) \equiv cz(a,b,c)≡c. Then the expression x2+y2+z2x^2 + y^2 + z^2x2+y2+z2 is a polynomial of linear operators of VVV. So the objects that are involved in the story are:
  • The vector space VVV of dimension nnn over the field kkk
  • The group GGG that acts on the vector space VVV
  • The space of linear operators V∗V^*V∗
  • The ring of linear operators RR R.
We can write any polynomial of the linear operators as polynomials of the 'elementary' projection operators p1,…,pnp_1, \dots, p_np1​,…,pn​ [where nnn is the dimension of VVV]. So we have that R≃k[p1,…,pn]R \simeq k[p_1, \dots, p_n]R≃k[p1​,…,pn​]. Now this ring RRR is said to be the ring of invariants of the action of the group GGG onto the vector space VVV.
