§ Solutions to system of equations

Note that we often define equations, for example x2+1=0x^2 + 1 = 0x2+1=0 over a ring such as Z\mathbb ZZ. But its solutions live elsewhere: In this case, the solutions live in C\mathbb CC, as well as in Z/2Z\mathbb Z/2ZZ/2Z. Hence, we should not restrict our solution space to be the ring where we defined our coefficients from! Rather, as long as we are able to interpret the polynomial f∈K[T]f \in K[\mathbf T]f∈K[T] in some other ring AAA, we can look for solutions in the ring AAA. § Equivalent systems of equations

Two systems of equations X,YX, YX,Y over the same ring KKK are said to be equivalent over KKK iff for all KKK-algebras LLL, we have Sol(X,L)=Sol(Y,L)Sol(X, L) = Sol(Y, L)Sol(X,L)=Sol(Y,L).

§ Biggest system of equations

For a given system of equations X≡(K[T],F)X \equiv (K[\mathbf T], \mathbf F)X≡(K[T],F) over the ring KKK, we can generate the largest system of equations that still has the same solution: generate the ideal F′=(F)\mathbf F' = (\mathbf F)F′=(F), and consider the system of equations X′≡(K[T],F′=(F))X' \equiv (K[\mathbf T], \mathbf F' = (\mathbf F) )X′≡(K[T],F′=(F)).

§ Varieties and coordinate rings

Let g∈K[T1,…Tn]g \in K[T_1, \dots T_n]g∈K[T1​,…Tn​]. The polynomial is also a function which maps x∈Kn\mathbf x \in K^nx∈Kn to KKK through evaluation g(x)g(\mathbf x)g(x). § An aside: why is it called the "coordinate ring"?

We can consider the ith coordinate function as one that takes K[T1,…,Tn]K[T_1, \dots, T_n]K[T1​,…,Tn​] to TiT_iTi​ So we have ϕi≡Ti∈K[T1,…,Tn]\phi_i \equiv T_i \in K[T_1, \dots, T_n]ϕi​≡Ti​∈K[T1​,…,Tn​] which defines a function ϕi:Kn→K\phi_i: K^n \rightarrow Kϕi​:Kn→K which extracts the iiith coordinate. Now the quotienting from the variety to build K[V]K[V]K[V], the coordinate ring of the variety VVV will make sure to "modulo out" the coordinates that "do not matter" on the variety.
