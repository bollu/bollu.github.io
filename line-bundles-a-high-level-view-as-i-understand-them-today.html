§ Why are modules invertible?

All modules are invertible when tensored with their dual. To simplify further, let's move to linear algbera from ring theory; consider the field R\mathbb RR. Over this, we have a vector space of dimension 111, R\mathbb RR. Now, if we consider R⊗R∗\mathbb R \otimes \mathbb R^*R⊗R∗, this is isomorphic to R\mathbb RR since we can replace (r,f)↦f(r)(r, f) \mapsto f(r)(r,f)↦f(r). This amounts to the fact that we can contract tensors. So, R⊗R∗≃R\mathbb R \otimes \mathbb R^* \simeq \mathbb RR⊗R∗≃R. Generalize to bundles.
