§ Display maps

  • Reference: Substitution on nlah
  • To to dependent types in a category, we can use display maps .
  • The display map of a morphism p:B→Ap: B \to Ap:B→A represents x:A∣−B(x):Typex:A |- B(x): Typex:A∣−B(x):Type. The intuition is that B(x)B(x)B(x)is the fiber of the map ppp over x:Ax:Ax:A.
  • For any category CCC, a class of morphisms DDD are called display maps iff all pullbacks of DDD exist and belong to DDD. Often, DDD is also closed under composition.
  • Said differently, DDD is closed under all pullbacks, as well as composition.
  • A category with displays is well rooted if the category has a terminal object 111, and all maps into 111are display maps (ie, they can always be pulled back along any morphism).
