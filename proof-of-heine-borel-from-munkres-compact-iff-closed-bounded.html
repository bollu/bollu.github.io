§ Proof of Heine Borel from Munkres (compact iff closed, bounded)

We wish to show that compact iff closed and bounded in R\mathbb RR.
  • Let SSS be closed and bounded. We wish to show that SSS is compact. Since SSS is bounded, SSS is contained in some large closed interval III. (1) Closed intervals are compact in the order topology of a complete total order, and thus III is compact ( R\mathbb RR is a complete total order). Then (2) SSS is a closed subset of a compact set III, and is thus compact. Hence closed and bounded implies compact.
  • Let SSS be compact. We wish to show that SSS is closed and bounded. Cover SSS with open balls of increasing radii. This is an open cover; Extract a finite subcover. From the finite subcover, pick the largest open ball III. SSS is entirely contained in III, and is thus bounded. (3) SSS is closed, as it is a compact subset of a Haussdorff space. Hence, we are done.

§ (2) Closed subset of a compact set is compact

Let BBB be a closed subset of a compact space KKK. Take an open cover {Ui}\{ U_i \}{Ui​} of BBB. See that {Bc,Ui}\{ B^c, U_i \}{Bc,Ui​} is a cover of the full space KKK, and hence has a finite subcover. This subcover will be of the form {Bc,Uj}\{B^c, U_j\}{Bc,Uj​}. The {Uj}\{U_j\}{Uj​} are a finite subcover of BBB. Thus, BBB is compact as we have extracted a finite subcover of an open cover.
