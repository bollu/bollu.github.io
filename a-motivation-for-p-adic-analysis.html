§ A motivation for p-adic analysis

I've seen the definitions of p-adic numbers scattered around on the internet, but this analogy as motivated by the book p-adic numbers by Fernando Gouvea really made me understand why one would study the p-adics, and why the definitions are natural. So I'm going to recapitulate the material, with the aim of having somoene who reads this post be left with a sense of why it's profitable to study the p-adics, and what sorts of analogies are fruitful when thinking about them. We wish to draw an analogy between the ring C[X]\mathbb C[X]C[X], where (X−α)(X - \alpha)(X−α) are the prime ideals, and Z\mathbb ZZ where (p)(p)(p) are the prime ideals. We wish to take all operations one can perform with polynomials, such as generating functions (1/(X−α)=1+X+X2+…1/(X - \alpha) = 1 + X + X^2 + \dots1/(X−α)=1+X+X2+… ), taylor expansions (expanding aronund (X−α)(X - \alpha)(X−α)), and see what their analogous objects will look like in Z\mathbb ZZ relative to a prime ppp.

Now, for example, given a prime ppp, we can write any positive integer mmm in base ppp, as (m=∑i=0naipi)(m = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i p^i)(m=∑i=0n​ai​pi) where (0≤ai≤p−1)(0 \leq a_i \leq p - 1)(0≤ai​≤p−1). For example, consider m=72,p=3m = 72, p = 3m=72,p=3. The expansion of 72 is 72=0×1+0×3+2×32+2×3372 = 0\times 1 + 0 \times 3 + 2 \times 3^2 + 2 \times 3^372=0×1+0×3+2×32+2×33. § What have we achieved so far?

We've now managed to completely reinterpret all the numbers we care about in the rationals as power series in base ppp. This is pretty neat. We're next going to try to complete this, just as we complete the rationals to get the reals. We're going to show that we get a different number system on completion, called Qp\mathbb Q_pQp​. To perform this, we first look at how the ppp-adic numbers help us solve congruences mod p, and how this gives rise to completions to equations such as x2−2=0x^2 - 2 = 0x2−2=0, which in the reals give us x=2x = \sqrt 2x=2​, and in Qp\mathbb Q_pQp​ give us a different answer!

§ Perspective: Forcing the formal sum to converge by imposing a new norm:

So far, we have dealt with infinite series in base ppp, which have terms pi,i≥0p^i, i \geq 0pi,i≥0. Clearly, these sums are divergent as per the usual topology on Q\mathbb QQ. However, we would enjoy assigning analytic meaning to these series. Hence, we wish to consider a new notion of the absolute value of a number, which makes it such that pip^ipi with large iii are considered small. § Conclusion

What we've done in this whirlwind tour is to try and draw analogies between the ring of polynomials C[X]\mathbb C[X]C[X] and the ring Z\mathbb ZZ, by trying to draw analogies between their prime ideals: (X−α)(X - \alpha)(X−α) and (p)(p)(p). So, we imported the notions of generating functions, polynomial evaluation, and completions (of Q\mathbb QQ) to gain a picture of what Qp\mathbb Q_pQp​ is like. We also tried out the theory we've built against some toy problems, that shows us that this point of view maybe profitable.
