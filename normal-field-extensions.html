§ Is normality of extensions transitivte?

  • Consider K⊆L⊆MK \subseteq L \subseteq MK⊆L⊆M. If K⊆LK \subseteq LK⊆L is normal, L⊆ML \subseteq ML⊆M is normal, then is K⊆MK \subseteq MK⊆M normal?
  • Answer: NO!
  • Counter-example: Q⊆Q(21/2)⊆Q(21/4)Q \subseteq Q(2^{1/2}) \subseteq Q(2^{1/4})Q⊆Q(21/2)⊆Q(21/4).
  • Each of the two pieces are normal since they are degree two. But the full tower is not normal, because Q(21/4)/QQ(2^{1/4})/QQ(21/4)/Q has minimial polynomial x4−2x^4 - 2x4−2.
  • On the other hand, Q(21/4)/Q(21/2)Q(2^{1/4})/Q(2^{1/2})Q(21/4)/Q(21/2) has a minimal polynomial x2−2∈Q[21/2]x^2 - \sqrt{2} \in Q[2^{1/2}]x2−2​∈Q[21/2].
  • So, normality is not transitive!
