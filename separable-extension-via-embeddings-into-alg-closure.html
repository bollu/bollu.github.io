§ Separable extension via embeddings into alg. closure

§ Defn by embeddings

  • Let L/KL/KL/K be a finite extension.
  • It is separable iff a given embedding σ:K→K‾\sigma: K \to \overline Kσ:K→K can be extended in [L:K][L:K][L:K] ways (This number can be at most [L:K][L:K][L:K].)
  • We call the numbe of ways to embed LLL in K‾\overline KK via extending σ\sigmaσ to be the separability degree of L/KL/KL/K.

§ At most [L:K][L:K][L:K] embeddings exist

§ Separability is transitive

  • Given a tower K⊆L⊆M⊆K‾K \subseteq L \subseteq M \subseteq \overline KK⊆L⊆M⊆K, we fix an embedding κ:K→K‾\kappa: K \to \overline Kκ:K→K. If both L/KL/KL/K and M/LM/LM/L are finite and separable, then κ\kappaκ extends into λ:L→K‾\lambda: L \to \overline Kλ:L→K through L/KL/KL/K in [L:K][L:K][L:K] ways, and then again as μ:L→K‾\mu: L \to \overline Kμ:L→K in [M:L][M:L][M:L] ways.
  • This together means that we have [L:K]⋅[M:L]=[M:K][L:K] \cdot [M:L] = [M:K][L:K]⋅[M:L]=[M:K] ways to extend κ\kappaκ into μ\muμ, which is the maximum possible.
  • Thus, M/KM/KM/K is separable.
