§ Thm: every free module is projective

  • Let PPP be a free module. Suppose we have an epimorphism e:M→Ne: M \to Ne:M→N and a morphism f:P→Nf: P \to Nf:P→N. We must create f~:M→N\tilde f: M \to Nf~​:M→N

§ (1 => 2): Projective as splitting of exact sequences

  • PPP is projective iff every exact sequence 0→N→M→πP→00 \to N \to M \xrightarrow{\pi} P \to 00→N→Mπ​P→0 splits.

§ (2 => 3): Projective as direct summand of free module

  • PPP is projective iff it is the direct summand of a free module. So there is a another module NNN such that P⊕N≡RnP \oplus N \equiv R^nP⊕N≡Rn.
  • We can always pick a surjective epi π:F→P\pi: F \to Pπ:F→P, where FFF is the free module over all elements of PPP.

§ Splitting lemma

  • If an exact sequence splits, then middle term is direct sum of outer terms.

§ Non example of projective module

  • Z/pZ is not projective.
  • We have the exact sequence 0 -> Z -(xp)-> Z -> Z/kZ -> 0 of multiplication by p.
  • This sequence does not split, because Z (middle) is not a direct summand of Z (left) and Z/kZ (right), because direct summands are submodules of the larger module. But Z/pZ cannot be a submodule of Z because Z/pZis torsion while Z is torsion free.

§ Example of module that is projective but not free

  • It's not free because any RnR^nRn will have 4n4^n4n elements, while PPP has only two element.
  • Geometrically, we have two points, one for each F2F_2F2​. The module PPP is a vector bundle that only takes values over one of the points. Since the bundle different dimensions over the two points (1 versus 0), it is projective but not free.
  • It is projective since it's like a vector bundle. It's not free because it doesn't have constant dimension.
