§ Principal curvature

  • take a point ppp. Consider the normal to the surface at the point, N(p)N(p)N(p).
  • Take any normal plane: a plane QpQ_pQp​ which contains N(p)N(p)N(p). This plane (which is normal to the surface, since it contains the normal) intersecs the surface SSS at a curve (intuitively, since a plane in 3D is defined by 1 eqn, intersection with plane imposes 1 equation on the surface, cutting it down to 1D).
  • The curvature of this curve (normal plane QpQ_pQp​ intersection surface SSS) at point ppp is the normal curvature of the normal plane QpQ_pQp​.
  • The maximum and minimum such normal curvatures at a point (max, min taken across all possible normal planes QpQ_pQp​) are the principal curvatures.
