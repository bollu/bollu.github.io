§ Part 1 + Part 2: Separable iff unique lift

  • Let L/KL/KL/K be separable. By primitive element theorem, L=K(α)L = K(\alpha)L=K(α) for some α∈L\alpha \in Lα∈L, α\alphaα separable over KKK.
  • Any derivation of KKK can be extended to a derivation of LLL from results above. Thus, separable implies unique lift.
  • Suppose L/KL/KL/K is inseparable. Then we can write L=F(α)/KL = F(\alpha)/KL=F(α)/K where α\alphaα is inseparable over FFF, and K⊆F⊆LK \subseteq F \subseteq LK⊆F⊆L.
  • Then by Part 2.a, we use the ZZZ derivation to non-zero derivation on LLL that is zero on FFF. Since it is zero on FFF and K⊆FK \subseteq FK⊆F, it is zero on KKK.
  • This shows that if L/KL/KL/K is inseparable, then there are two ways to lift the zero derivation, violating uniqueness.

§ Lemma: Derivations at intermediate separable extensions

  • Let L/KL/KL/K be a finite extension, and let F/KF/KF/K be an intermediate separable extension. So K⊆F⊆LK \subseteq F \subseteq LK⊆F⊆L and F/KF/KF/K is separable.
  • Then we claim that every derivation D:F→LD: F \to LD:F→L that sends KKK to KKK has values in FFF. (ie, it's range is only FFF, not all of LLL).
  • Pick α∈F\alpha \in Fα∈F, so α\alphaα is separable over KKK. We know what the unique derivation looks like, and it has range only FFF.

§ Payoff: An extension L=K(α1,…,αn)L = K(\alpha_1, \dots, \alpha_n)L=K(α1​,…,αn​) is separable over KKK iff αi\alpha_iαi​ are separable
