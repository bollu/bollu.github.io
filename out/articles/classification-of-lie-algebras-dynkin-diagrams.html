§ Classification of complex lie algebras

  • LLL is a complex vector space with a lie bracket [.,.][., .][.,.].

§ Theorem (Leri)

  • Every finite dimensional complex Lie algebra (L,[.,.])(L, [.,.])(L,[.,.]) can be decomposed as L=R⊕s(L1⋯⊕Ln)L = R \oplus_s (L_1 \dots \oplus L_n)L=R⊕s​(L1​⋯⊕Ln​), where ⊕\oplus⊕is direct sum, ⊕s\oplus_s⊕s​ is the semidirect sum.
  • RRR is a solvable lie algebra.
  • To define solvable, define R0=RR_0 = RR0​=R, R1=[R0,R0]R_1 = [R_0, R_0]R1​=[R0​,R0​], R2=[R1,R1]R_2 = [R_1, R_1]R2​=[R1​,R1​], that is, R2=[[R,R],[R,R]]R_2 = [[R, R], [R, R]]R2​=[[R,R],[R,R]].
  • We have that Ri+1R_{i+1}Ri+1​ is a strict subset of RiR_iRi​.
  • If this sequence eventually stabilizes, ie, there is an nnn such that Rn={0}R_n = \{ 0 \}Rn​={0}, then RRR is solvable.
  • In the decomposition of LLL, the RRR is the solvable part.
  • We have L1L_1L1​, \dots, LnL_nLn​ which are simple. This means that LiL_iLi​ is non-abelian, and LiL_iLi​ contains no non-trivial ideals. An ideal of a lie algebra is a subvevtor space I⊆LI \subseteq LI⊆L such that [I,L]⊆I[I, L] \subseteq I[I,L]⊆I. (It's like a ring ideal, except with lie bracket).
  • The direct sum L1⊕L2L_1 \oplus L_2L1​⊕L2​ of lie algebras is the direct sum of vector spaces with lie bracket in the bigger space given by [L1,L2]=0[L_1, L_2] = 0[L1​,L2​]=0.

§ Remarks

  • It is very hard to classify solvable Lie algebras.
  • A lie algebra that has no solvable part, ie can be written as L=L1⋯⊕LnL = L_1 \dots \oplus L_nL=L1​⋯⊕Ln​ is called as semi-simple .
  • It is possible to classify the simple Lie algebras.
  • We focus on the simple/semi-simple Lie algebras. Simple Lie algebras are the independent building blocks we classify.

§ Adjoint Map

  • Let (L,[.,.])(L, [., .])(L,[.,.]) be a complex lie algebra. Let h∈Lh \in Lh∈L be an element of the lie algebra.
  • Define ad(h):L→Lad(h): L \to Lad(h):L→L as ad(h)(l)≡[h,l]ad(h)(l) \equiv [h, l]ad(h)(l)≡[h,l]. Can be written as ad(h)≡[h,−]ad(h) \equiv [h, -]ad(h)≡[h,−]. This is the adjoint map wrt h∈Lh \in Lh∈L.

§ Killing form

  • K:L×L→CK: L \times L \to \mathbb CK:L×L→C is a bilinear map, defined as K(a,b)≡tr(ad(a)∘ad(b))K(a, b) \equiv tr(ad(a) \circ ad(b))K(a,b)≡tr(ad(a)∘ad(b)).
  • See that ad(a)∘ad(b):L→Lad(a) \circ ad(b): L \to Lad(a)∘ad(b):L→L. the trace will be complex because LLL is complex.
  • Since LLL is finite dimensional vector space, trtrtr is cyclic. So tr(ad(a)∘ad(b))=tr(ad(b)∘ad(a))tr(ad(a) \circ ad(b)) = tr(ad(b) \circ ad(a))tr(ad(a)∘ad(b))=tr(ad(b)∘ad(a)). This means that K(a,b)=K(b,a)K(a, b) = K(b, a)K(a,b)=K(b,a), or that the killing form is symmetric!
  • Cartan criterion: LLL is semi-simple iff the killing form KKK is non-degenerate. That is, K(a,b)=0K(a, b) = 0K(a,b)=0 implies b=0b = 0b=0.

  • Thm Any finite dimensional lie algebra possesses a cartan subalgebra.

  • ad(h)(eα)=λα(h)eαad(h)(e_\alpha) = \lambda_\alpha(h) e_\alphaad(h)(eα​)=λα​(h)eα​.

  • How to recover all roots from fundamental roots?
  • For any λ∈Phi\lambda \in Phiλ∈Phi, define the Weyl transformation sλ:HR⋆→HR⋆s_\lambda: H^\star_R \to H^\star_Rsλ​:HR⋆​→HR⋆​

§ Theorem: Roots are prouced by action of Weyl group on fundamental roots

  • Any λ∈Φ\lambda \in \Phiλ∈Φ can be produced by the action of some w∈Ww \in Ww∈W on some π∈Π\pi \in \Piπ∈Π.
