  • Intuition: the gcd represents the common roots of l,rl, rl,r in Zariski land. That is, if l,rl, rl,r are zero at a prime then so is r−lr - lr−l.
  • So, the GCD equally well represents the common roots of lll and (r−l)(r - l)(r−l).
  • Now, if a number xxx vanishes at a subset of the places where yyy vanishes, we have x<yx < yx<y (the prime factorization of yyy contains all the prime factors of xxx).
  • Since the GCD vanishes at the subset of the roots of lll, a subset of the roots of rrr, and a subset of the roots of (r−l)(r-l)(r−l), it must be smaller than all of these.
  • Thus, the GCD is at most r−lr - lr−l.
