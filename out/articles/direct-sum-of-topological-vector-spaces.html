§ Direct sum of topological vector spaces

  • In vector spaces, direct sum (also direct product) needs projection functors π1,π2:V→X,Y\pi_1, \pi2_: V \to X, Yπ1​,π2:​V→X,Ysuch that X×Y=VX \times Y = VX×Y=V.
  • In topological vector spaces, these projections also need to be continuous which is a massive thing to ask for.

  • Let XXX be a Hilbert space with schrauder basis (topological basis) e[i]e[i]e[i]
  • Consider subspaces spanned by the basis A[k]≡e[2k]A[k] \equiv e[2k]A[k]≡e[2k], and B[k]≡e[2k]+e[2k+1]/(k+1)B[k] \equiv e[2k] + e[2k+1]/(k+1)B[k]≡e[2k]+e[2k+1]/(k+1). So A≡span(A[k])A \equiv span(A[k])A≡span(A[k]), B≡span(B[k])B \equiv span(B[k])B≡span(B[k]).
