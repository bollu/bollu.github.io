§ Disjoint Coproduct

  • One says that a coproduct X+YX+YX+Y is disjoint iff the intersection of XXX with YYY in X+YX+YX+Y is empty.
  • The intersection of A,BA, BA,B over XXX is defined as the pullback of the diagram (in fact, cospan) A→X←BA \rightarrow X \leftarrow BA→X←B.
  • Thus, in this case, we say that X,YX, YX,Y are disjoint iff the pullback of X→X+Y←YX \rightarrow X+Y \leftarrow YX→X+Y←Y is the initial object.
