§ Vandermonde and FFT

FFT lets you do the following: You are given two sequences: a0,...,an and b0,...,bm Compute sequence c0,…,c[n+m] such that
c[i]=a[0]b[i]+a[1]b[i−1]+⋯+a[i−1]b[1]+a[i]b[0]c[i]=a[0]b[i]+a[1]b[i−1]+ \dots +a[i−1]b[1]+a[i]b[0]c[i]=a[0]b[i]+a[1]b[i−1]+⋯+a[i−1]b[1]+a[i]b[0]
This is exactly what I needed here, where a[i]=(ni)a[i]= \binom{n}{i}a[i]=(in​) and b[i]=(mi)b[i]= \binom{m}{i}b[i]=(im​). If I thought for a little longer I would realize that c[i]=(n+mi)c[i]=\binom{n+m}{i}c[i]=(in+m​) instead of computing it using FFT.
