§ In a PID, all prime ideals are maximal, geometrically

Assume RRR is Noetherian.
  • By Krull's principal ideal theorem,we have that given a principal ideal I=(α)I = (\alpha)I=(α), all minimal primeideals p\mathfrak pp above III has height at most 1.
  • Recall that a minimal prime ideal p\mathfrak pp lying over an ideal IIIis the minimal among all prime ideals containing III. That is, ifI⊆q⊆pI \subseteq \mathfrak q \subseteq \mathfrak pI⊆q⊆p, then q=I\mathfrak q = Iq=Ior q=p\mathfrak q= \mathfrak pq=p.
  • In our case, we have that RRR is a PID. We are trying to show that all primeideals are maximal. Consider a prime ideal p⊆R\mathfrak p \subseteq Rp⊆R.It is a principal ideal since RRR is a PID. It is alsoa minimal prime ideal since it contains itself. Thus by Krull's PID theorem, has height at most one.
  • If the prime ideal is the zero ideal (p=0\mathfrak p = 0p=0),then it has height zero.
  • If it is any other prime ideal (p≠(0))(\mathfrak p \neq (0))(p=(0)), then it has heightat least 1, since there is the chain (0)⊊p(0) \subsetneq \mathfrak p(0)⊊p. Thusby Krull's PID theorem, it has height exactly one.
  • So all the prime ideals other than the zero ideal, that is, all the pointsof Spec(R)Spec(R)Spec(R) have height 1.
  • Thus, every point of Spec(R)Spec(R)Spec(R) is maximal, as there are no "higher points"that cover them.
  • Hence, in a PID, every prime ideal is maximal.
