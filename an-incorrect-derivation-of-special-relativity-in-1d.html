§ The assumption

We assume that the velocity of light as measured by any inertial frame is constant. Thus if x,x′x, x'x,x′ are the locations of light as measured by two inertial frames, and t,t′t, t't,t′ is the time elapsed as measured by two inertial frames, we must have that dx/dt=dx′/dt′dx/dt = dx'/dt'dx/dt=dx′/dt′. This ensures that the speed of light is invariant.

§ What's the issue?

The issue is the equation x′=vt+ctx' = vt + ctx′=vt+ct.
  • It is true that as per observer (1) standing at the origin, the distancebetween observer (2) and the photon is vt+ctvt + ctvt+ct.
  • It is not true that observer(2) sees the distance between them and the photon as vt+ctvt + ctvt+ct.
  • Intuitively, this equation of x′=vt+ctx' = vt + ctx′=vt+ct completely ignores lengthcontraction, and hence cannot be right.
  • Alternatively, the equation of x′=vt+ctx' = vt + ctx′=vt+ct imposes galilean relativity, where I am attempting to naively connect reference frames, which cannot becorrect.
