§ Maximal Ideals of Boolean Algebras are Truth Values

§ Boolean algebras

  • Has meet, join, complement, 1, 0 with usual laws

§ Atomic boolean algebras

  • Consider 2S2^S2S where SSS is finite. Then the elements of the form {s} ∈ 2^S are said to be atoms because if x ⊂ {s} then x = 0 or x = {s}.

§ Atomless boolean algebras

  • Let SSS be an infinite set, and let III be a collection of its finite subsets. Then III is an ideal (downward closed subset which has all joins), because the union of two finite sets is finite, and the subset of any finite set is finite.
  • The quotient T=2S/IT = 2^S/IT=2S/I will be an atomless boolean algebra.
  • Note that the quotient kills all finite subsets.
  • So for any non-zero x∈Tx \in Tx∈T, then it must be an equivalence class with some infinite subset. If we take k,k′k, k'k,k′ to be non-empty disjoint subsets of xxx, then neither is equivalent to xxx or to ∅\emptyset∅, because they differ at infinitely many locations from each. Thus, xxx is not an atom.

§ Or, how to embed a poset into a boolean algebra.

  • Every poset PPP can be embedded into a complete atomic boolean algebra 2P2^P2Pby sending p↦{x:x≤p}p \mapsto \{ x : x \leq p \}p↦{x:x≤p} (the ideal of ppp).
  • Alternatively, that's just Hom(−,p)Hom(-, p)Hom(−,p). God bless yoneda embedding.
  • We can thus consider a ring map from 2p→22^p \to 22p→2, which gives us a maximal ideal of 2P2^P2P (ideal is maximal because quotient is field).
  • This assigns to us consistent truth values of ppp.
  • In this way, maximal ideals of posets completed to rings correspond to truth values.
