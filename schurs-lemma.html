§ Strengthing the theorem: what is fff?

We can additionally show that if fff is not the zero map, then fff is constant times the identity. That is, there exists a λ\lambdaλ such that f=λIf = \lambda If=λI.
  • fff cannot have two eigenvalues. If it did, the eigenspaces of λ1\lambda_1λ1​ and lambda2lambda_2lambda2​ would be different subspaces that are stabilized by fff. This can't happen because VVV is irreducible. So, fff has a single eigenvalue λ\lambdaλ.
  • Thus, if fff has full spectrum, it's going to be f=λIf = \lambda If=λI.
  • fff has full spectrum since we tacitly assume the underlying field is C\mathbb CCand fff has full rank.
